# The Pushing Medium (PM) Framework — Introduction

The Pushing Medium framework asserts that space is a physical medium with definable internal
properties. This medium possesses density, compression, and flow, and these quantities evolve
according to physical laws. Space is not an empty geometric background; it is a material
substrate whose state determines the behaviour of matter and radiation. Because all physical
systems are composed of the same medium, no internal experiment can identify a preferred rest
frame.

## Matter as a Localised Configuration of the Medium

Matter is interpreted as a stable, localised configuration of the Pushing Medium. It is not a
void, cavity, or separate substance embedded within the medium. Instead, it corresponds to a
region in which the medium occupies a distinct internal state. The boundary of such a region
is not a membrane in the conventional sense, but the transition zone between the internal
configuration and the ambient medium.

This configuration perturbs the surrounding medium. The medium responds to this perturbation
by adjusting its density and compression fields, establishing a stable equilibrium between
internal and external pressures. The resulting non‑uniformity in the medium's state is the
origin of long‑range gravitational effects.

## Gravity as the Medium's Equalisation Response

The Pushing Medium tends toward uniformity. A matter configuration disrupts this uniformity,
and the medium responds by attempting to equalise the disturbance. This response generates:

- spatial gradients in the compression field ϕ,
- flows of the medium described by a velocity field **u**,
- and pressure forces acting on all excitations of the medium.

These pressure forces constitute gravity. In this framework, gravity is not an attractive pull
and not a manifestation of spacetime curvature. It is the dynamical consequence of the
medium's attempt to restore equilibrium around a localised disturbance.

## Light as an Excitation Propagating Through the Medium

Light is treated as an excitation of the Pushing Medium whose propagation speed depends on the
medium's local state. Variations in the compression field ϕ modify the effective refractive
index n = e^ϕ. Light therefore follows trajectories determined by spatial variations in this
index. Gravitational lensing arises as a refraction phenomenon rather than as geodesic motion
in curved spacetime.

## What PM is Not

**Not an ether theory.**
The medium is not a substance sitting inside a background space. It *is* the substrate. There
is no preferred rest frame that can be detected by internal excitations, because all
instruments and particles are made of the same medium.

**Not a flat‑space force law.**
PM does not add a new force to Newtonian space. The medium has its own density, flow, and
dynamics. Space is not a passive stage; it is an active physical system.

**Not a geometric theory.**
PM does not use curvature, metrics, geodesics, or tensor gravity. The mathematics is that of
a compressible medium, not a curved manifold.

## Why PM is Not Just a Reinterpretation of GR

PM makes different predictions in the strong field:

- PM has no photon sphere.
- PM shadows are surface‑set, not universal.
- PM compact objects do not produce GR‑style photon rings.
- PM predicts different behaviour for ultra‑compact stars and black‑hole candidates.

## Conceptual Coherence and Physical Interpretation

The PM framework places gravity within the same conceptual category as other physical
interactions. It provides:

- a substrate with definable physical properties,
- a mechanism for gravitational acceleration,
- a unified origin for matter, inertia, and light propagation,
- and a consistent interpretation of long‑range forces as pressure‑driven responses.

The model avoids the shortcomings of historical ether theories by eliminating any background
space distinct from the medium. It avoids the abstractions of geometric gravity by replacing
curvature with physical dynamics. It also avoids the limitations of flat‑space force laws by
assigning the medium its own internal degrees of freedom and evolution equations.

## Summary

- Matter is a stable configuration of the Pushing Medium.
- Gravity is the medium's pressure‑driven response to the disturbance created by that configuration.
- Light is an excitation whose propagation is governed by the medium's local state.

This framework provides a unified, mechanistic account of gravitational phenomena without
invoking geometric curvature or separate force fields.
