\section{Core Assumptions}
%====================================================================

\begin{enumerate}\setlength{\itemsep}{2pt}
  \item \textbf{Flat spacetime.}
        Space is a compressible physical medium on a fixed Euclidean background.
        No GR curvature, no Einstein equations, no horizons, no TOV.

  \item \textbf{Scalar compression field.}
        Gravity arises from the scalar field $\phi$ with refractive index
        $n=e^{\phi}$.  Sources: $\nabla^2\phi = -\tfrac{8\pi G}{c^2}\rho$.

  \item \textbf{Density law.}
        \[ \rho = \rho_{\mathrm{nuc}}\,e^{\phi} \]

  \item \textbf{Force law for massive particles.}
        \[ \mathbf{a} = \tfrac{c^2}{2}\,\nabla\phi \]
        (Half the photon coupling; recovers $-GM/r^2\,\hat{\mathbf{r}}$ exactly.)

  \item \textbf{Light propagation.}
        Follows Fermat's principle in the optical metric:
        \[ ds_{\mathrm{opt}}^2 = -\frac{c^2}{n^2}\,dt^2
           + (d\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{u}\,dt)^2 \]

  \item \textbf{Self-consistent EOS.}
        \[ P = \frac{c^2}{2}\!\left(\rho - \rho_{\mathrm{nuc}}\right) \]

  \item \textbf{Stability cap.}
        The medium cannot be compressed beyond $\phi = 1$ ($n = e$,
        $\rho = e\,\rho_{\mathrm{nuc}}$).  This is the only strong-field limit
        in PM; it is not a compactness bound from GR.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection*{Diagnostic: Three-Way Disconnection of $U(\phi)$}
%--------------------------------------------------------------------

The deformation energy $U(\phi)$ is currently used \emph{only} as a
stability condition.  Three structural gaps exist.

\textbf{Gap~1 — $U(\phi)$ is absent from the Lagrangian.}
The PM Lagrangian density (see §PM Lagrangian) contains gradient and
kinetic terms $-\tfrac{1}{2}|\nabla\phi|^2 + \tfrac{(\partial_t\phi)^2}{2c_\phi^2}$
but no potential term $-V(\phi)$.  Consequently the static equation of
motion for $\phi$ is the linear Poisson equation
\[
  \nabla^2\phi = -\tfrac{8\pi G}{c^2}\rho_M,
\]
with \emph{no self-coupling from $U$}.  The stability cap $\phi<1$ is
therefore an externally imposed condition, not a dynamical consequence
of the Lagrangian.  If $U$ were included as $-V(\phi)=-U(\phi)/c_\phi^2$,
the field equation would become
\[
  \nabla^2\phi \;-\; \frac{U'(\phi)}{c_\phi^2}
  = -\tfrac{8\pi G}{c^2}\rho_M
  \qquad\bigl[U'(\phi)=\varepsilon_0(2\phi-\phi^2)\bigr],
\]
a nonlinear Poisson equation that
would self-regulate compression and make $\phi_{\rm crit}=1$ an emergent
feature rather than a postulate.

\textbf{Gap~2 — $U(\phi)$ and the EOS are thermodynamically inconsistent.}
If $U(\phi)$ were the fluid's internal energy density, the thermodynamic
pressure would follow from $P_{\rm thermo}=\rho^2\partial(U/\rho)/\partial\rho$.
Evaluating with $\rho=\rho_{\rm nuc}e^{\phi}$:
\[
  P_{\rm thermo}
  = \varepsilon_0\!\left(2\phi - 2\phi^2 + \frac{\phi^3}{3}\right).
\]
At small $\phi$ this gives $P_{\rm thermo}\approx 2\varepsilon_0\phi$, whereas
the stated EOS gives $P_{\rm EOS}=\tfrac{c^2}{2}(\rho-\rho_{\rm nuc})
\approx\tfrac{\varepsilon_0}{2}\phi$.
The ratio $P_{\rm thermo}/P_{\rm EOS}\approx 4$ at lowest order.
They are measuring fundamentally different things and are never
reconciled in the current framework.  $U(\phi)$ is thus \emph{not}
the thermodynamic free energy of the PM fluid — its physical
interpretation is unspecified.

\textbf{Gap~3 — Field gradient energy $\tfrac{1}{2}|\nabla\phi|^2$
does not gravitate.}
The Hamiltonian (see §Hamiltonian) contains $\tfrac{1}{2}|\nabla\phi|^2$
as positive-definite field energy, yet the Poisson equation sources
$\phi$ from $\rho_M$ alone.  In the strong-field interior:
\[
  |\nabla\phi|^2 \sim \left(\frac{\mu_G m(r)}{r^2}\right)^2.
\]
At the PM stability cap this omitted self-energy density is
$\lesssim 1\%$ of $\rho_{\rm crit}c^2$, so it is negligible for
stellar structure — but it is a formal inconsistency that would
become important if $U(\phi)$ is modified to allow higher compactness.

\textbf{Root cause.}  All three gaps trace to the same origin:
$U(\phi)$ was introduced as a phenomenological stability condition
(analogous to a Landau order parameter) without being derived from
the PM Lagrangian.  Because it feeds into neither the Poisson
equation nor the hydrostatic equilibrium, the compact-star sector
rests on a stability criterion that is decoupled from the dynamics.
This is the most likely site of the strong-field aberration.

\textbf{Minimal repair strategy.}
A self-consistent extension requires choosing \emph{one} of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item[(a)] Add $-U(\phi)/c_\phi^2$ to the Lagrangian; re-derive the EOS
        from the modified equation of motion; accept that
        $P_{\rm EOS}$ changes.
  \item[(b)] Keep the EOS and the Lagrangian as-is; replace $U(\phi)$ with
        a different stability argument whose critical point $\phi_c$
        is a free parameter fit to compact-object data (effectively
        relaxing the hard cap).
  \item[(c)] Interpret $U(\phi)$ as an \emph{additional} elastic pressure
        of the medium, add it to the hydrostatic equilibrium
        ($P_{\rm total}=P_{\rm EOS}+U'(\phi)\times$(geometric factor)),
        and require thermodynamic self-consistency.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Numerical probe results (gap\_probe\_comparison.py).}
Running the PM compact-star ODE under each repair option yields:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Gap~2 — additional finding:}
    $P_{\rm thermo}(\phi)$ is \emph{non-monotonic}.  Its maximum occurs at
    \[
      \frac{dP_{\rm thermo}}{d\phi} = \varepsilon_0(2-4\phi+\phi^2) = 0
      \;\Longrightarrow\;
      \phi_{\rm max} = 2-\sqrt{2} \approx 0.586 < \phi_{\rm crit}=1.
    \]
    By $\phi_{\rm crit}=1$ the thermodynamic pressure has \emph{decreased}
    to $P_{\rm thermo}(1)=\tfrac{1}{3}\varepsilon_0 < 0.80\,P_{\rm thermo}(\phi_{\rm max})$.
    Hence $U(\phi)$ cannot serve as a thermodynamic free energy anywhere
    in the range $[0.586, 1]$ — the derived ``EOS'' would have negative
    compressibility.

  \item \textbf{Gap~1 — Poisson correction magnitude (corrected formula):}
    The correct Gauss-theorem result for the $U'(\phi)$ self-coupling is
    \[
      \delta\!\left(\frac{d\phi}{dr}\right)
      = \frac{\mu_G}{r^2}\int_0^r \frac{U'(\phi(r'))}{c^2}\,r'^2\,dr'
      \approx \mu_G\,\rho_{\rm nuc}(2\phi-\phi^2)\,\frac{r}{3}.
    \]
    (The na\"ive formula $(r/3)U'(\phi)/c^2$ omits $\mu_G=2G/c^2$ and has
    unit $\mathrm{kg\,m^{-2}}$ instead of $\mathrm{m^{-1}}$, inflating the
    estimate by $\sim 10^{23}$.)
    The correctly normalised ratio at $\phi=\phi_{\rm crit}$ with interior
    density $\bar\rho\approx\rho_{\rm crit}$ is
    \[
      \frac{\delta(d\phi/dr)}{(d\phi/dr)_{\rm std}}
      \approx \frac{\rho_{\rm nuc}}{4\pi\,\rho_{\rm crit}}
      = \frac{1}{4\pi e} \approx 2.9\%.
    \]
    \emph{The Gap~1 Poisson correction is a percent-level effect in the
    stellar interior — not negligible at precision level, but far smaller
    than the Gap~3 elastic pressure ($\sim 78\%$ of $P_{\rm EOS}$).}

  \item \textbf{Option~(b) — negative result:}
    Sweeping $\phi_{\rm crit}$ from 1 to 5 with the baseline linear EOS
    ($c_s = c/\sqrt{2}$) does \emph{not} raise $M_{\rm max}$ above 30\,M$_\odot$
    for any value in that range.  The mass ceiling is determined by EOS
    stiffness, not by the stability cap.  \emph{Option~(b) alone cannot
    close the BH mass gap.}

  \item \textbf{Option~(c) — positive result:}
    Setting $P_{\rm total}=P_{\rm EOS}+U(\phi)$ at unchanged $\phi_{\rm crit}=1$
    raises $M_{\rm max}$ from ${\approx}14\,M_\odot$ (baseline) to
    ${\approx}31\,M_\odot$ — comparable to the GW150914 lower component
    ($30.7^{+5.2}_{-3.0}\,M_\odot$).  Mechanism: $U(\phi_{\rm crit})=
    \tfrac{2}{3}\varepsilon_0 \approx 78\%\times P_{\rm EOS}(\rho_{\rm crit})$
    substantially stiffens the effective EOS.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Gap~4 — $\rho_{\rm nuc}$ as the critical radius variable (from test\_rho\_nuc\_constraint.py).}
After Option~(c) fixes $M_{\rm max}$, the residual problem is the radius:
$R_{1.4}\approx 14.0$\,km (ODE) vs.\ $\leq 13.3$\,km (GW170817, 90\% CI).
Further probing reveals the root cause:

The PM stellar surface condition is $P=0 \Leftrightarrow \rho=\rho_{\rm nuc}$.
A $1.4\,M_\odot$ star has mean density $\langle\rho\rangle \approx 0.11\,\rho_{\rm nuc}$,
so the star is nearly a \emph{uniform sphere at density $\rho_{\rm nuc}$}:
\[
  \boxed{R_{1.4} \approx \left(\frac{3\,M_{1.4}}{4\pi\,\rho_{\rm nuc}}\right)^{1/3}
        = 14.24\;\text{km}}     \qquad (\text{ODE: }14.0\pm 0.1\;\text{km})
\]
For this reason, $R_{1.4}$ is insensitive to both $\alpha$ (the U$(\phi)$
coefficient) and $\gamma$ (the EOS sound-speed factor $c_s^2=\gamma c^2/2$):
both modify the EOS at densities well above $\rho_{\rm nuc}$, whereas the
star's bulk is near $\rho_{\rm nuc}$.  Numerical confirmation:
$R_{1.4}$ varies $\leq 4\%$ over $\alpha\in[0,2]$ and $\leq 8\%$ over $\gamma\in[0.3,2]$.

The \emph{only} parameter that directly controls $R_{1.4}$ is $\rho_{\rm nuc}$:
\[
  R_{1.4}\propto \rho_{\rm nuc}^{-1/3},
  \qquad
  \frac{\partial \ln R_{1.4}}{\partial \ln\rho_{\rm nuc}} = -\tfrac{1}{3}
  \;\;\text{(confirmed to 0.2\%)}
\]

\textbf{Two-parameter repair and joint feasibility window.}
Defining $f=\rho_{\rm nuc}/\rho_{\rm nuc,0}$, the two observational constraints:
\begin{align*}
  M_{\rm max}&\geq 28\,M_\odot \quad\text{(requires $\alpha\sim 1$, i.e.\ Option~c)}\\
  R_{1.4}&\leq 13.3\;\text{km}\quad\text{(requires $f\geq 1.18$)}
\end{align*}
are \emph{decoupled}: $\alpha$ controls $M_{\rm max}$ at high $\phi$,
$f$ controls $R$ via the surface condition.  Both are simultaneously satisfiable:

The joint feasibility window is narrow ($\approx 3$\% in $\rho_{\rm nuc}$)

\textbf{Summary of findings.}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $M_{\rm max}$: controlled by $U(\phi)$ at high $\phi$ (Option~c).  Fixed.
  \item $R_{1.4}$: controlled by $\rho_{\rm nuc}$ (the surface density anchor).
        Requires a $\sim 18\%$ upward revision of $\rho_{\rm nuc}$.
  \item The two corrections are decoupled and jointly feasible.
  \item $U(\phi)$ cannot serve as a thermodynamic free energy above $\phi=0.586$.
\end{enumerate}

Option~(a) changes the most and preserves formal consistency.
Options~(b) and~(c) are more conservative.  Any repair must preserve
the weak-field limit ($U\to 0$ as $\phi\to 0$) and the two-phase
cosmological structure ($\phi_{\rm crit}$ couples to $\Omega_E$).
